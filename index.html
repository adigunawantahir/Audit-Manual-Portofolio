1. The Evolving Role of the Auditor

2. Comprehensive Audit Framework (Workflow)

  • Risk-Based Strategic Planning: The initial stage for identifying critical areas requiring audit focus.
  • Disciplined Fieldwork Execution: Applying consistent and professional methodologies during evidence gathering.
  • Impactful Reporting: Presenting findings and recommendations in a strategic and actionable manner.
  • Systematic Follow-Up Monitoring: Ensuring management takes effective and timely corrective actions.

3. Quality Assurance and Improvement Program (QAIP)

  1. Internal Quality Review: Involves ongoing monitoring of the audit activity's performance (e.g., through supervision) and periodic internal assessments.
  2. Evaluation: These formal assessments evaluate the function's conformance with IIA Standards, the Code of Ethics, and its overall effectiveness in achieving its mandate.

4. External Quality Assessment (EQA)

5. Competency and Continuous Development

  • Continuous Professional Development (CPD): Mandatory ongoing training.
  • Professional Certifications: Attaining recognized qualifications (such as the Certified Internal Auditor - CIA).
  • Self-study: Proactive, independent learning to keep pace with industry changes.
